The Life of a Genius

Early Years

Wiley’s childhood experiences in the South Central neighborhood of Los Angeles were enriched by his mother’s passion for education. At the age of 11, he took art classes at a conservatory at California State University, and at 12 years old he attended a six-week art program outside Leningrad.

Earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree (1999) at the San Francisco Art Institute and a Master of Fine Arts (2001) at the School of Art at Yale University. While in residence in 2001 at the Studio Museum in Harlem, he found a discarded New York City Police Department.

He followed those with his breakthrough Passing/Posing series (2001–04), in which he replaced the heroes.

Timeline of Achievements

1999–2005

Artistic Awakening

Graduated from Yale School of Art (MFA, 2001). Developed his signature style of reimagining classical portraiture with contemporary Black subjects. First gained attention with Passing/Posing series (2003).

2005–2012

Global Recognition

Began his World Stage project, creating portraits across 13 countries from Brazil to Nigeria. Painted Napoleon Leading the Army Over the Alps (2005), reinterpreting Jacques-Louis David's work with a modern Black equestrian.

2013–2017

Presidential & Institutional Impact

Commissioned to paint President Barack Obama's official portrait (2018) for the Smithsonian National Portrait Gallery—the first Black artist to do so. Founded Black Rock Senegal, an artist residency program in Dakar (2019).

2018–2021

Monumental Works & Public Art

Created Rumors of War (2019), a 27-foot bronze sculpture in Times Square as a counterpoint to Confederate monuments. Expanded into stained glass with Saint Adelaide (2020) for the Rothko Chapel.

2022–Present

Cultural Legacy & Innovation

Featured in major retrospectives worldwide. Continues to blend Old Master techniques with Afrofuturism, exploring themes of power, race, and queer identity through increasingly multimedia works.
