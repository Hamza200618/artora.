The Life of a Genius

Early Years

Yayoi Kusama (born March 22, 1929, Matsumoto, Japan) is a Japanese artist who is a self-described “obsessional artist,” known for her extensive use of polka dots and for her infinity installations. She employed painting, sculpture, performance art, and installations in a variety of styles, including Pop art and Minimalism.

By her own account, Kusama began painting as a child, at about the time she began experiencing hallucinations that often involved fields of dots.

Those hallucinations and the theme of dots would continue to inform her art throughout her career. She had little formal training, studying art only briefly (1948–49) at the Kyōto City Specialist School of Arts.

Timeline of Achievements

1957–1972

New York Avant-Garde Period

Moved to New York and became a central figure in the avant-garde art scene. Created iconic works like Infinity Nets paintings and Accumulation sculptures. Held provocative happenings and body-painting events in protest of the Vietnam War.

1973–1988

Return to Japan & Literary Career

Returned to Tokyo and checked herself into a psychiatric hospital, where she still lives today. Published several novels and poetry collections, including The Hustler’s Grotto of Christopher Street. Shifted focus to writing while continuing to create art in seclusion.

1989–1999

International Rediscovery

Regained global attention with exhibitions in New York and Europe. Created her first Infinity Mirror Rooms, which became her signature installations. Represented Japan at the Venice Biennale (1993).

2000–2015

Pop Culture Icon Status

Collaborated with Louis Vuitton (2012) and other major brands, bringing her polka dots to fashion. Opened the Yayoi Kusama Museum in Tokyo (2017). Her Infinity Rooms broke attendance records worldwide.

2016–Present

Legacy & Global Domination

Named one of Time magazine’s 100 most influential people. Continued producing large-scale exhibitions, including Infinity Mirrors touring show. At 95, she remains one of the most celebrated and highest-grossing living artists.
