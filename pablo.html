Pablo

The Life of a Genius

Early Years

Pablo Picasso (1881-1973) was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist, and theatre designer, widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century. He co-founded the Cubist movement, invented constructed sculpture, and co-invented collage.

Picasso's artistic journey began in Spain and later flourished in France, where he developed a wide range of styles and techniques throughout his prolific career.

His father, José Ruiz y Blasco, was an art teacher and instilled in him a love for art from a young age.

Timeline of Achievements

1881–1901

Early Life & Blue Period

Born in Málaga, Spain, Picasso moved to Barcelona and later Paris. During this time, he developed his somber Blue Period, inspired by poverty and emotional struggles.

1904–1906

Rose Period

Began using warmer colors and circus themes in his works during his Rose Period, reflecting a more optimistic outlook after settling in Montmartre, Paris.

1907–1914

Birth of Cubism

Co-founded the Cubist movement alongside Georges Braque and produced groundbreaking works such as Les Demoiselles d’Avignon, radically changing modern art.

1917–1936

Classical Period & Experimentation

Explored neoclassical styles and collaborated with the Ballets Russes, while continuing to experiment with surrealist elements and various artistic techniques.

1937–1973

Political Works & Late Masterpieces

Created the iconic anti-war painting Guernica (1937) and continued to produce prolific artworks throughout his later life, becoming one of the most influential artists of the 20th century.
