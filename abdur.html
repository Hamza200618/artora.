The Life of a Genius

Early Years

Radia Durrani is a visual artist and Illustrator based in Karachi, Pakistan. She graduated from The Indus Valley School of Art and Architecture with a Bachelor's degree in Communication Design in 2020. Since then, she has participated in group exhibits in Pakistan and worked on several projects including creating illustrations for Aga Khan University’s digital magazine.

She currently works as a graphic designer and Illustrator at DAWN media group.Radia’s work is rooted in realism. Her illustrations are both digital and traditional, with water colours being her favourite choice of medium.

Her passion and craft lies in her eye for detail and rendering multiple layers to achieve a hyper realistic quality in her paintings as well as her digital illustrations.

Timeline of Achievements

2008–2012

Karachi Foundation Years

Studied at the Indus Valley School of Art and Architecture. Developed her signature style blending Mughal miniature techniques with contemporary themes. Held her first solo exhibition, Whispers of Identity, at the Canvas Gallery.

2013–2016

Lahore Renaissance

Relocated to Lahore, collaborating with poets at the Faiz Festival. Created the Veiled Narratives series, exploring female agency in Pakistani folklore. Won the National Arts Council’s "Emerging Artist Award" (2015).

2017–2019

International Recognition

Featured in the South Asian Modern exhibition at London’s Saatchi Gallery. Pioneered the use of digital-augmented reality (AR) in traditional Pakistani art. Conducted workshops at the Lahore Biennale.

2020–2022

Activism Through Art

Launched the Silent Echoes project, documenting climate change’s impact on Thar’s nomadic communities. Partnered with UNESCO to preserve Sindhi textile motifs in her murals.

2023–Present

Legacy & Innovation

Appointed curator at the Pakistan National Art Museum. Working on The Unseen Archive, a multimedia retrospective of Pakistan’s feminist art movements.
